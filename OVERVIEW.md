# Overview

A web platform that allows amateur football players to relive, watch, and share their match highlights shortly after the game ends.

The platform focuses on video-first experience, player identity, and simple content sharing for tournaments and local leagues.

---

## Problem

Amateur players rarely have access to professional-style match highlights.

Most local tournaments either:
- Do not record games at all, or
- Upload full matches without easy access to key moments.

Players want to:
- Rewatch their best plays
- Share goals and saves with friends and family
- Build a personal sports identity

But current solutions are either too expensive, too complex, or not player-focused.

---

## Target User

- Amateur football players
- Local tournament participants
- Small football leagues
- Friends and family of players
- Young athletes building a sports portfolio

---

## Why This Project?

Perfect for learning modern product building because it includes:

- Structured navigation (tournaments → matches → highlights)
- Video-based UI
- Card-based layouts
- Dynamic routing (player and match pages)
- State management
- Filtering and sorting
- Local data persistence
- Mobile-first design

It combines product thinking, UX design, and frontend implementation in a real-world sports scenario.
